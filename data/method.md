# The method

*This is the front-page "How I know it's the right person" section, in my own voice.*

*The working spec lives in `RULES-AND-INSTRUCTIONS.md` — that one is the instructions I hold myself and the assistant to. This one is the same rules, written as I'd explain them to whoever is reading the site. If the rules change, change both.*

---

## THE STANDING RULE

> **Everything that has held up came from an original record image.**
> **Everything that misled came from an Ancestry hint or someone else's tree.**
>
> **Method: Clue → hypothesis → document → fact. Never clue → fact.**

I open the register image. I read the abode, the occupation, the fathers, the witnesses. *Then* I decide. Other people's trees and bare transcriptions are leads to test — they are not evidence, and I don't treat them as any.

---

## THE HARD RULES

1. **I work the direct line, one generation at a time**
   Straight up from myself, and I don't climb until the ancestor below is properly sourced and both his parents are actually there. No brothers, no sisters, no cousins on this pass — every direct ancestor, on every branch, and nothing else. It's slower. It's the only way I don't end up hanging a whole family off the wrong man.

2. **I add it and I source it there and then**
   The moment a record is confirmed, every fact off it goes on the profile and the document goes on with it. I never type a date in by hand when the record exists — I attach the record. And I attach the awkward ones too: the second marriage, the husband's death that made her a widow, the occupation, the burial, the passage out to Canada. The tree is the deliverable. The worklist is not.

3. **I check it before, and I check it after**
   Before I accept anything I read the whole record and tick every person and every fact I mean to take from it. After I save, I open the Facts page and make sure it all actually landed — Ancestry drops things quietly and doesn't tell you. And I confirm from the image that it is my person: the spouse, the address, the father's name. I am not merging a stranger into this family because the name matched.

4. **Nobody is finished until the whole life is there**
   Birth or baptism, marriage, death or burial, and every census they lived through — plus the military papers, the probate, the emigration, where they apply. "It's got some hints" is not done. "Parents confirmed" is not done. **Added is not the same as sourced.** A grandmother who turned up in her son's census still needs records of her own before I call her finished.

---

*Where a life should have left a record and hasn't, this site says so. It doesn't fill the hole with a plausible guess.*
